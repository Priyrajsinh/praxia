## Worked example — the one-sample _t_-statistic

This is the engine behind the [Foundations](/foundations) → Data Analyst statistics work. To test
whether a sample mean $\bar{x}$ differs from a hypothesised population mean $\mu_0$, you standardise
the gap by the **estimated standard error** of the mean:

$$
t = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s / \sqrt{n}}
$$

where $s$ is the sample standard deviation and $n$ the sample size. Under the null hypothesis the
statistic follows a $t$-distribution with $n - 1$ degrees of freedom — and _why_ it does (the
sampling distribution of the mean, the central limit theorem) is exactly the kind of derivation the
research-grade column will carry.
